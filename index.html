Charts show how the adapter evolved: what it absorbed, when its direction settled, and when the spike formed.

direction stability — is it still learning, or done?
cos(checkpoint, previous). Climbing = still finding the concept. Pinned at ~1 = concept locked; every step after only changes volume, not content.
total Δ norm — how loud the adapter is
Total weight change. Flattening = saturated (learning finished). Still climbing after the direction has locked = amplifying the same thing louder — the overcook mechanism.
peak σ_max — spike watch
Strongest single direction in any layer. Over the dashed ceiling (~5) = will conflict with other adapters when stacked; the higher it climbs, the worse.
median stable rank — memorization watch
How many directions carry the update. Steady/high = learning general features. Falling = collapsing onto few directions = memorizing the dataset.
spectrogram — where the energy lives
Each row a layer, each column a checkpoint. Teal = healthy strength; orange rows forming = a spike growing in that layer (krea2 LoRAs: watch the gate rows). Clicking a column opens that checkpoint in Inspect — navigation, not a judgement.
